About me

I’m a Master of Science student at American University in Washington, D.C., pursuing an M.S. in Data Science (expected May 2026). My academic background combines statistical rigor with applied analytics; coursework includes Statistical Methods, Regression, Statistical Programming in R, and Business Insights/Analytics.

Prior to graduate school, I earned a B.S. in Data Science from Penn State University. I also worked as a Data Scientist at Koch Industries in the FP&A division, supporting financial forecasting and building models to inform strategic decisions. Most recently, I’ve focused on projects involving crime trend analysis, environmental behavior modeling, and interactive dashboards using R, Python, SQL, and Power BI.

I’m passionate about leveraging data to solve complex business and social problems—especially in financial analytics, operational efficiency, and policy impact.
